Discover Ibadan

Visiting a city for the first time can be difficult and confusing, Here are some of the most exciting places in Ibadan you should be eager to visit. They include historical sites, cultural buildings, mall and even a university.

Days Since Last Visits:

Cocoa House

Cocoa House, completed in 1965 at a height of 105 metres, was once the tallest building in Nigeria and the first skyscraper in West Africa. It is located in Dugbe, a major commercial area in Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria. It was built from proceeds of agricultural commodities (e.g., Cocoa, Rubber, Timber) of the then Western State of Nigeria.

Asejire Reservoir

The Asejire Reservoir is in Oyo State in the south west of Nigeria on the Osun River, about 30 kilometers east of Ibadan. The reservoir was built in the late 1960s. The water supply project was completed in 1972, and has a capacity of about 80 million liters per day, of which 80% is used for domestic purposes.

Obafemi Awolowo Stadium

The Obafemi Awolowo Stadium located at Liberty road, Ring Road in Ibadan, Nigeria, originally known as Liberty Stadium until 2010 is a football stadium with a capacity of 25,000 seats. The stadium was opened in 1960 during the tenure of Chief Obafemi Awolowo who was serving as the Premier of the Western Region at the time.

Agodi Gardens

Formerly called Agodi Zoological and Botanical Gardens, Agodi Gardens was created in 1967. The garden was destroyed by the Ogunpa flood disaster in 1980 as most of the animals were swept away by the raging water. The garden was renovated in 2012 by the Oyo State Government and the new Agodi Gardens was reopened in 2014.

Ilaji Resort

Sited on an expansive land mass of over 300 Acres. A Resort with everything you need from hospitality, sports and entertainment facilities all in one place. It is way off on the heart out the city which is not bad. Ilaji Hotels and Sports Resort features a restaurant, bar, a garden and private beach area in Ibadan.

Mapo Hall

The Mapo hall is another iconic place you definitely should visit in Ibadan. Not only is the building a view to behold, it is an edifice built during the colonial era by Captain Ross in 1929. It was designed and constructed by engineer Robert Jones betwwen 1925-1929.

University of Ibadan

Although being a federal university, it resides in the Agbowo area of Ibadan and can be a great place to visit. The department of Art does a great job to litre the school campus with wonderful sculptures they make from their projects and they look pleasing to the eyes
